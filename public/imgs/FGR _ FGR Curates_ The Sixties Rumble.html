The Global Sound Revolution

The 1960s wasn't just a decadeit was a global revolution in sound. From the British Invasion that conquered American airwaves to the psychedelic experiments of San Francisco, from the bossa nova rhythms of Brazil to the ska explosion in Jamaica, music was breaking boundaries and creating new worlds.

This September, FGR takes you on a journey through the most influential musical movements of the 1960s. Each week, we'll explore a different region and its unique contribution to the global soundscape. Through curated playlists, rare footage, and deep dives into the cultural contexts that shaped these sounds, we'll trace how the music of the 60s continues to influence everything we listen to today.

Join us at Beachville Coffee Roasters every week as we celebrate the weird music for weird people that defined a generation and changed the world.
