The 60s across South America wasn’t exactly smooth sailing — coups, dictatorships, and Cold War puppet-strings pulled tight. The U.S. had its hands deep in the region, usually to stop anything smelling vaguely of socialism. That tension meant music wasn’t just entertainment; it was also rebellion, survival, and in some cases, propaganda. Artists either leaned into government-approved “wholesome” sounds or used rhythm and poetry to slip messages past the censors.
